CLM Misting Solutions offers support to DIY'ers and professional installers to help with the installation of our system. The high-level overview below facilitates the installation process and can be customized based on your expertise. Our goal is to provide full coverage, long-term reliability, and optimal performance—whether for residential backyards, horse barns, or outdoor dining areas.

  • Site Evaluation & Planning
    • Determine optimal coverage areas.
    • Inspect the property to identify mosquito breeding and resting sites.
    • Customize the layout for maximum effectiveness.
  • System Design & Nozzle Placement
    • Place nozzles every 8 to 10 feet along fences, shaded areas, and eaves.
    • Select tubing colors that blend in with the surroundings (black, white, grey, brown, or tan).
  • Professional Installation & Testing
    • Wall mount the misting system or use a tank mount if wall mounting is not optimal.
    • Place the 55-gallon poly drum reservoir directly below the unit for maximum performance.
    • Test the system to ensure proper mist distribution and adjust as needed.
    • Provide customer training for confident system operation.
  • ✅ Residential Properties – Protects patios, pools, and yards.
  • ✅ Barns & Equine Centers – Controls flies and mosquitoes in horse stalls.
  • ✅ Outdoor Dining & Restaurants – Ensures a mosquito-free experience.
  • ✅ Event Spaces – Perfect for weddings, venues, and resorts.
  • 🏡 Clear Your Space: Move furniture or obstacles from misting areas.
  • 📦 Take Inventory: Ensure you have all necessary tools (tubing cutters, PVC pipe if burying line, shovel, zip ties, fencing, misting system components, nozzles, tee connectors, risers, elbows, etc.).
  • 🔧 Act Like a Professional: Space out nozzles 8 to 10 feet apart before connecting the tubing. Dig trenches under gates and for risers if needed.
  • 🛠 Final Testing: Once installation is complete, test the system to ensure optimal performance and adjust coverage as needed.
  • 📖 Customer Training: Learn how to operate, refill, and maintain your system.
